Universities I've studied in

The Escuela de Ingeniería y Arquitectura (EINA) at the University of Zaragoza (Unizar) is one of Spain’s leading technical schools, offering a wide range of engineering and architecture degrees. Located at the Río Ebro Campus in Zaragoza, it provides modern facilities, research centers, and strong industry connections. EINA is known for its focus on innovation, actively participating in national and European research projects in areas such as artificial intelligence, robotics, energy, and telecommunications.
The Computer Engineering degree at EINA is one of its most popular programs, preparing students in software development, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and computer systems. The program combines theoretical knowledge with practical applications, ensuring graduates are well-equipped for careers in technology, research, and industry. With a strong emphasis on internships and collaborations with leading companies, EINA’s Computer Engineering degree is recognized for producing highly skilled professionals in the field.

University College Cork (UCC) is one of Ireland’s leading universities, renowned for its research, innovation, and academic excellence. Founded in 1845 and located in Cork, UCC is part of the National University of Ireland and consistently ranks among the top institutions globally. It offers a vibrant campus life, state-of-the-art facilities, and strong industry connections, making it a hub for students pursuing various disciplines, including science, engineering, business, and the humanities.
The Computer Science degree at UCC is a highly regarded program that provides students with a strong foundation in software development, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and data science. With a curriculum that balances theoretical knowledge and practical experience, students gain hands-on skills through projects, labs, and industry placements. UCC has strong ties with leading tech companies, ensuring graduates are well-prepared for careers in the ever-evolving technology sector. The program also emphasizes research and innovation, offering opportunities for students to engage in cutting-edge developments in computing.
UCC is also famous for its connection to George Boole, the mathematician and logician known as the father of Boolean algebra, which laid the foundations for modern computing. Boole was the first professor of mathematics at UCC (then called Queen’s College Cork) and made groundbreaking contributions to logic and algebra, which are fundamental to computer science today.
